Types of Food Menus
 

Food Menus are a list of what you want to order in a restaurant.

8 Types of Food Menus:

    ===================================================

  1. Table d'hote

    2. - A pre-set, multi-course meal offered at a fixed price. It typically includes an appetizer,
    main course, and dessert, with a limited number of options for each course.

    ===================================================

  2. A la Carte

    3. - This menu refers to when a diner orders one type of dish that is separate from a plate of an original menu.

    ===================================================

  3. Static Menu

    4. - This is a larger menu, typically divided into categories, that doesn't change very often.
    It's the most widely used menu today, and it's what you likely think of when you think of menus.

    ===================================================

  4. Cycle Menu

    5. - This menu is a series of menus that is repeated over a specific period of time, such as 4 weeks. The menu is different
    each day during the cycle. And, At the end of the cycle, the menu is repeated.

    ===================================================

  5. Single-Use Menu

    6. - Also called a disposable menu—is any menu designed to be used only once.
    After they're used, they're recycled or thrown out.

    ===================================================

  6. Tourist Menu

    7. - This Menu is nothing special, this menu is only used to attract tourist with traditional cuisines etc.

    ===================================================

  7. Californian Menu

    8. - This is a type of À la carte menu often served in hotels or inns.

    ===================================================
